Travel with me

I grew up around travel, but not quite in it. As a baby, I lived in multiple places due to my dad’s work, visiting different countries along the way. But, I remember very little of it, and for most of my adult life, travel wasn’t really part of my world.

That began to change in the last couple of years. With independence came opportunity—and curiosity. Once I started traveling on my own, I understood what I’d been missing. The movement, the pause from routine, the way places linger with you.

In 2025, after getting my driver’s license, travel took a new form: long road trips—slower, deeper, and more intentional. This page is a collection of those journeys: places we loved, routes we drove, and recommendations from journeys that stayed with me longer than I expected.

Along the way, we’ve found ourselves gravitating toward nature— parks, and open landscapes that make the journey feel as meaningful as the destination.

Places I’ve been

Pacific Overlook, Golden Gate Bridge

This spot, right by the Golden Gate Bridge, overlooks the vast Pacific Ocean. It’s one of the prettiest views where the city’s landmark meets the open sea.
View on Google Maps

Huntington Beach Pier

Sunset at Huntington Beach, where dozens of surfers caught the last waves of the day. Just steps away, the Tuesday farmer’s market filled the air with local flavors and energy.
View on Google Maps

Pelican Cove Park, Rancho Palos Verdes

Pelican Cove Park offers striking cliffs and endless blue waters, a perfect coastal vista. We stopped here while driving up the coast from Orange County and were rewarded with this incredible scenery.
View on Google Maps

Orange Hills, Orange County

We drove up Orange Hills and were greeted with sweeping views of the city below. From there, the sunset lit the sky with a perfect round orange sun, true to its name.
View on Google Maps

Golden Gate Bridge

The iconic Golden Gate Bridge view, with the Marin Headlands in the distance.
View on Google Maps

Fisherman’s Wharf, San Francisco

Sunset over Fisherman’s Wharf, just down from Ghirardelli Square.
View on Google Maps

Muir Beach, California

Muir Beach is a quiet stretch of sand along the Marin County coast, framed by rugged cliffs and rolling hills. A peaceful stop just north of San Francisco, it’s perfect for catching ocean views.
View on Google Maps

Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk

Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk had that perfect old-school arcade and carnival vibe, with train rides and games all around. The mix of seaside fun and ocean views and of course the sunsets.
